Conditions we treat

Clinical care for the realities of aging

Our licensed therapists treat the most common mental-health needs in senior living — with assessment, ongoing therapy, and family support built in.

Depression

Late-life depression often shows up as withdrawal, sleep disruption, low energy, or somatic complaints — not the textbook presentation. We assess, treat, and coordinate with the medical team using evidence-based therapy adapted for older adults.

Anxiety disorders

Generalized anxiety, panic, health anxiety, and worry about family, finances, or independence. We use cognitive-behavioral and acceptance-based approaches calibrated to age-related concerns and any cognitive changes.

Anger management

Irritability, frustration, and outbursts often signal underlying pain, fear, or loss of control. We help residents identify triggers, build coping skills, and reduce conflict with caregivers and family.

Behavior problems

Resistance to care, agitation, wandering, and disruptive behaviors. We work with residents and your care team to identify causes, adjust environmental triggers, and apply behavior-management strategies that protect everyone's dignity.

Grief & loss

Loss of a spouse, friends, siblings, or one's prior self. Complicated grief, anticipatory grief, and bereavement are common in senior living — and respond well to therapy when addressed directly.

Memory loss

Mild cognitive impairment and early-to-moderate dementia respond to reminiscence work, supportive therapy, and behavioral interventions. We coordinate with care teams and family, and adapt our approach as needs change.

Transition & adjustment disorder

Moving into a community, leaving a long-time home, role changes, loss of independence — all real, all treatable. We help residents process the transition and find meaning in this chapter, not just cope with it.

Post-surgical mental health

Recovery brings pain, fear, frustration, and sometimes depression. We support residents through orthopedic, cardiac, oncologic, and other post-surgical recovery — keeping them engaged in rehab and on track to baseline.

Post-traumatic stress

Past combat, abuse, accidents, or medical trauma can resurface in late life. Trauma-informed therapy — including evidence-based PTSD modalities — helps residents process and reduce symptom burden at any age.

Chronic disease coping

Cancer, cardiovascular disease, chronic pain, COPD, post-stroke adjustment. We help residents process the emotional weight of chronic illness, manage pain-related distress, and stay engaged in their medical care.

Drug & alcohol dependency

Substance-use issues in older adults are under-recognized and treatable. We provide assessment, motivational and cognitive-behavioral therapy, and coordination with medical providers — confidentially and without stigma.
